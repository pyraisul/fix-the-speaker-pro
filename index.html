How Does the Speaker Cleaner Work?

A quick look at why sound waves are surprisingly good at pushing out water and dust

🌊

Water Ejection Technology

We play a 165Hz tone — the same low-frequency range Apple uses on the Apple Watch water lock — to get your speaker's diaphragm moving fast enough to physically push water droplets out through the grille.

Think of it like tapping the side of a glass to move bubbles. The speaker membrane vibrates rapidly, and the trapped moisture has nowhere to go but out. No disassembly, no waiting overnight, no bag of rice.

💨

Dust Removal Process

Dust cleaning works a bit differently. Instead of one steady tone, we sweep through frequencies from 200Hz up to 6400Hz. Each frequency targets a different particle size, so everything from fine pocket lint to gritty sand gets shaken loose.

It's basically a sonic shower for your speakers — the vibrations reach deep into the grille where a toothbrush or compressed air can't. Safe for your hardware, and genuinely effective at clearing buildup.

📳

Vibration Enhancement

Sometimes sound alone isn't enough — especially if your phone took a full dunk. Vibration mode adds physical haptic pulses on top of the audio frequency, giving stubborn water droplets an extra nudge.

The combo of low-frequency sound + rapid vibrations works really well on phones that have been sitting wet for a while. Run it right after the water eject cycle for the best results.

How to Fix Your Speaker — Step by Step

1

Prepare Your Device

Grab a dry cloth and wipe off any visible water on the outside. Then crank your phone volume all the way up — louder means stronger vibrations.

2

Place on a Flat Surface

Lay your phone speaker-side down on a flat table or counter. Gravity helps pull the water out while the sound pushes it from the inside.

3

Pick Your Mode

Hit "Eject Water" if your phone got wet, or "Remove Dust" if the sound is just muffled from gunk. You can also target the left speaker, right speaker, or both.

4

Start Cleaning

Tap the "Start Cleaning" button and let it run until the progress bar fills up. You'll hear a steady tone — that's the cleaning frequency doing its thing.

5

Try Vibration Mode

Once the sound cycle finishes, switch over to vibration mode for an extra round of cleaning. It helps shake out any leftover moisture or debris.

6

Test & Repeat if Needed

Play some music or make a quick call to check the sound. Still a bit off? Run through the cycle again — most people see a big improvement after 2-3 rounds.

Tips to Get the Best Results

A few things that make a real difference during the cleaning process

💡

Max Out Your Volume

This one's critical — higher volume means the speaker membrane moves more aggressively, which is exactly what you need to push water and dust out.

💡

Speaker Side Down

Flip your phone so the speakers face the surface. Gravity does half the work when the sound waves start pushing moisture downward and out.

💡

Wipe the Outside First

Use a soft cloth to pat down any visible water before you start. That way the sound waves focus on the moisture trapped inside the speaker, not the puddle on the surface.

💡

Don't Wait Around

The sooner you run the cleaner after water exposure, the better. Water sitting in your speaker for hours can seep deeper and do real damage.

💡

Run Multiple Rounds

One cycle is good, but 2-3 rounds is better. Alternate between water eject and vibration mode — each pass pulls out a bit more moisture.

💡

Test Between Rounds

Play a song or call someone after each cycle. You'll be able to hear the improvement and judge whether another round is worth it.

💡

Sound + Vibration Together

For tough cases, switching between audio cleaning and vibration mode gives you the best shot. Sound waves handle the easy stuff, vibration shakes loose whatever's left.

💡

Make It a Habit

You don't have to wait until something sounds wrong. Running a quick clean once a week keeps your speakers in top shape and prevents buildup from getting bad.

How to Keep Your Speakers Healthy

A few habits that'll save you from speaker problems down the road

🛡️ Use Protective Cases

A decent waterproof case goes a long way, especially if you're often around pools, beaches, or just the kitchen sink. Some cases even have built-in speaker covers that block water without killing the sound.

⚠️ Avoid High-Risk Environments

We all bring our phones everywhere, but try to be mindful near water. If you know you'll be poolside or at the beach, a cheap waterproof pouch can save you a lot of trouble.

🧼 Regular Cleaning

A quick speaker clean once a week takes less than a minute. It prevents the kind of gradual dust and lint buildup that sneaks up on you and eventually makes everything sound muddy.

💧 Act Fast on Spills

If your phone gets wet, don't just shrug it off for later. Power it down if you can, and run the water eject tool right away. The faster you act, the less likely the water is to cause lasting damage.

🏠 Control Environment

Don't leave your phone sitting in a steamy bathroom or a hot car. Humidity slowly works its way into every opening. Tossing a silica gel packet in your nightstand drawer isn't a bad idea either.

🔍 Regular Inspections

Take a look at your speaker grilles now and then. If you can see dust or gunk building up, give it a gentle brush and follow up with our dust removal mode for the stuff you can't reach.
